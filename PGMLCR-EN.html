Requirements Catalog for Perishable Goods Inventory and Storage Management Systems

Introduction

This site serves as an official repository to present a structured requirements catalog aimed at information systems for the inventory and storage management of perishable goods in the Brazilian logistics sector. The artifact consolidates functional, non-functional, and legal requirements extracted from regulatory evidence, designed to accelerate the development of software solutions and ensure technical compliance in this domain.

Software Engineering and the creation of robust systems are fundamental to the sector. In the development of logistics management systems, the precise definition of requirements is the key element ensuring the software meets operational and regulatory needs (ROBERTSON; ROBERTSON, 2012). There is a growing demand to create information systems capable of integrating operational and environmental data to support decision-making in the management of sensitive inventories, as the digitalization of storage processes is a key factor in reducing waste and improving efficiency (BALLOU, 2006; GHIANI et al., 2022).

Logistical complexity is intensified by the strict prevailing regulatory framework. The effectiveness of storage and transportation inherently depends on compliance with sanitary legislation imposed by the National Health Surveillance Agency (ANVISA) and the Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Food Supply (MAPA). Ensuring that software complies with these regulations—such as temperature verification, traceability, and equipment calibration—not only guarantees the health and safety of the final consumer but also acts as an essential mitigator against commercial losses and legal liability in the supply chain.

The Catalog

The development of the catalog was based on qualitative applied research, guided by the engineering research method (WIERINGA, 2014; FELDERER & TRAVASSOS, 2020). The process was structured in the stages of Elicitation, Development, and Evaluation, combining a systematic literature review and a deep documentary analysis of ANVISA and MAPA regulations to identify, extract, and formalize the requirements.

The purpose of this catalog is to act as a concrete and reusable knowledge artifact. It can be used both as a technical foundation and guide for developers to design and accelerate the development of new software in the logistics area—aligning operational needs with legal compliance—and as an analytical tool for auditing and evaluating storage systems already operating in the market.

To ensure clarity and the adoption of good Requirements Engineering practices, the specification was divided. Functional Requirements, which dictate the technical behaviors and actions of the system, were structured and modeled using the use case methodology based on the Volere template. Non-Functional Requirements, responsible for designating quality goals (such as usability and security) and legal constraints of the application, are mapped using the iNFR framework, ensuring standardization and traceability.

Functional Requirements

Specification Card: RF-EST-01
Requirement No.
RF-EST-01
Name
Batch and Expiration Date Control
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must mandatorily perform individual tracking of each item or load unit present in the inventory via the batch number. For each entry record, the software must require and store the manufacturing date and the expiration date corresponding to the batch, preventing the completion of the process if these fields are not filled.
Justification (Standard)
Ensure traceability and monitoring of product movement along the supply chain, as required by Joint Normative Instruction (INC) ANVISA/MAPA No. 02/2018 [1].
Fit Criterion
The system must block the dispatch of any item that does not have a linked batch or whose expiration date is not registered in the database.
Dependencies
RF-CAD-01 (Product Base Registration).
Interdependencies
Related to RF-EST-02 for executing the FEFO policy (expiration).
Priority
Essential.
History
Created on 2026-03-18 by Samuel Celso for compliance with the socio-biodiversity framework.
Specification Card: RF-EST-02
Requirement No.
RF-EST-02
Name
Support for Multiple Movement Policies
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must allow the configuration of inventory output algorithms, mandatorily supporting the following policies: FIFO (First In, First Out); FEFO (First Expire, First Out), which automatically recommends batches with the closest expiration date; and Weighted Average Cost for accounting purposes.
Justification (Reference)
Optimize the management of perishable products to reduce waste and financial losses due to expiration, based on principles of logistics efficiency.
Fit Criterion
When initiating a dispatch order, the system must automatically prioritize at the top of the list the items whose expiration dates expire first (FEFO).
Dependencies
RF-EST-01 (requires registered expiration data).
Interdependencies
Directly impacts the accuracy of management indicators described in RF-REL-01.
Priority
Essential.
History
Included in the catalog on 2026-03-18 to support technologically advanced operations management.
Specification Card: RF-EST-05
Requirement No.
RF-EST-05
Name
Storage Conditions Monitoring (IoT)
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must integrate with temperature and humidity sensors (IoT) installed in the warehouse. The software must continuously receive and record this data, displaying values in real-time and issuing automatic audio or visual alerts if levels deviate from the predefined safety range for the product.
Justification (Standard)
Ensure compliance with conservation specifications and documented process control, according to ANVISA RDC No. 18/2013, Art. 139 [2].
Fit Criterion
The system must generate an audit log proving the thermal stability of the environment throughout the batch's entire storage period.
Dependencies
RNF-INT (Interoperability with hardware/sensor APIs).
Interdependencies
Provides critical data for legal requirement RL-002 (Maintenance of Equipment and Cold Rooms).
Priority
Essential.
History
Proposed on 2026-03-18 in response to the need for real-time monitoring of sensitive inventories.
Specification Card: RL-002
Requirement No.
RL-002
Name
Equipment Maintenance Record
Classification
Legal (LR)
Detailed Description
The system must provide a specific module to record all maintenance and calibration activities for critical equipment (cold rooms, thermometers, scales). The record must contain the date, responsible technician, description of the service performed, and the scheduling of the next inspection.
Justification (Standard)
Perform scheduled and periodic maintenance of equipment and calibration of measuring instruments, keeping records according to ANVISA RDC No. 216/2004, Art. 4.1.16 [3].
Fit Criterion
The system must automatically send a notification to managers 15 days before the expiration of any mandatory calibration.
Dependencies
RF-CAD-01 (Asset/Equipment Registration).
Interdependencies
Complements RF-EST-05, ensuring the sensors monitoring the inventory are properly calibrated.
Priority
Essential.
History
Added to mitigate sanitary non-compliance risks during official audits.

Name
Origin Registration with Geolocation
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must allow detailed registration of the origin of each raw material batch. This includes the identification of the producer or extractivist and the mandatory capture of geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude) of the collection point or rural property where the product originated.
Justification (Standard)
Meet the mandatory requirement to identify the origin and producers in the supply chain, as established by Joint Normative Instruction (INC) ANVISA/MAPA No. 02/2018 [1].
Fit Criterion
Batch registration must be blocked if geolocation data or supplier identification is not provided.

Supply Chain Tracking (End-to-End)
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must chronologically record all movements and transformations of a batch along the supply chain. Events such as collection, physical movements between locations, processing stages, storage periods, and final distribution must be recorded.

Enable complete tracking of a product's movement along the supply chain, recording origin and movements, according to INC ANVISA/MAPA No. 02/2018 [1].

It must be possible to generate a complete history (log) of every hand the batch has passed through, from extraction to dispatch.

Specification Card: RF-REL-01
Requirement No.
RF-REL-01
Name
Control Panels (Dashboards)
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must feature interactive visual panels (dashboards) consolidating Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) of logistics operations. Dashboards must display metrics on inventory turnover, perishable goods loss rate, inventory accuracy, and service level provided.
Justification (Standard)
The digitalization of storage processes is a key factor to reduce waste and improve service levels in perishable supply chains. Logistics efficiency directly depends on the ability to control material flows sustainably.
Fit Criterion
Dashboards must allow data filtering by period, storage unit, and product category (e.g., socio-biodiversity).
Dependencies
RF-EST (Inventory Management) and RF-RAS (Traceability) for movement data supply.
Interdependencies
Uses data collected via IoT (RF-EST-05) to generate alerts for losses due to thermal deviations.

Specification Card: RF-REL-02
Requirement No.
RF-REL-02
Name
Compliance Reports
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The system must be capable of automatically generating reports consolidating all records required by ANVISA and MAPA audits. The document must group facility sanitation logs, equipment maintenance and calibration schedules, and the complete traceability history of each batch.
Justification (Standard)
The system must support regulatory compliance by automating controls to prevent failures compromising quality and safety. Meets mandatory cleaning and maintenance record requirements outlined in ANVISA RDC 216/04 [3] and Portaria 326/97 [5].
Fit Criterion
The generated report must be in a non-editable format (PDF) and contain the digital signature or unambiguous identification of the person responsible for the record.
Dependencies
RF-RAS-02 (Supply Chain Tracking), RL-002 (Equipment Maintenance), and sanitation records (as per Table 1 of the catalog).
Interdependencies
Ensures the auditability of input data provided by Supplier (RF-FOR) and Storage modules.

RF-WMS-01
Name
Product Allocation and Segregation Rules
Classification
Functional
Detailed Description
The Warehouse Management System (WMS) must support and automatically apply allocation rules preventing the joint storage of incompatible products (such as cleaning materials and food) or those requiring specific isolation.
Justification (Standard)
Ensure food is stored in a way that prevents contamination, according to ANVISA Portaria 326/97 [5], and ensure chemical products are kept isolated from production areas, following MAPA IN No. 05/2017, § 5 [6].
Fit Criterion
The system must block any attempt to move a chemical product into a storage zone defined as "Food" or "Perishables".

Validation

The creation of a requirements catalog, no matter how detailed, requires a validation process to ensure its correctness, completeness, and relevance to the application domain. As described by Felderer and Travassos (2020), the use of a qualitative and interactive method, such as conducting Focus Group sessions with experts—ensuring the perspective of different chain stakeholders—acts to capture rich perceptions, verifying if the catalog's priority reflects the business reality.

Therefore, we invite professionals with experience in Software Engineering, sanitary regulation experts, as well as managers in the productive and logistics sectors to critically evaluate the requirements provided here. Your evaluations regarding technical precision, completeness against standards, and operational adequacy will help continuously improve the guidelines, solidifying this artifact for reuse in industry projects.
